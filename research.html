Terahertz Intensity Mapper (TIM) & SPIDER

[cite_start]At the Observational Cosmology & Particle Astrophysics Lab (UIUC), I work in cryogenic instrumentation and analysis for balloon-borne cosmological telescopes[cite: 13, 23]. My primary hardware focus is the Terahertz Intensity Mapper (TIM), where I worked on the characterization of Terahertz absorbers and a terahertz reflective attentuator using a Toptica Terascan 1550 frequency-selectable laser source. I engineered a blackbody paddle (macrobolometer) for checking [cite_start]infrared loading and worked on developing ultra-thin UHMWPE vacuum windows optimized for mechanical robustness[cite: 23]. I further played a key role in seeing [cite_start] the optical response of Kinetic Inductance Detector (KID) arrays during instrument first light.[cite: 10].

I address the challenge of satellite interference the SPIDER CMB telescope. For the SPIDER II mission, I developed the an astrometry package. This tool tracks Starlink satellite constellations to identify transient signatures in detector timestreams. It includes functionality to cross-correlate [cite_start]satellite positions with CMB data (in Healpix coordinates)[cite: 21, 22].

Caltech SURF : Observational Cosmology Group

Institution: Caltech  •  Dates: June 2025 – Aug 2025  •  Advisors: Late Dr. Kenny Lau, Prof. James Bock

As part of Caltech's Observational Cosmology Group, I commissioned a 100mK Adiabatic Demagnetization Refrigerator (ADR) testbed for future CMB and line intensity mapping detectors. I installed and optimized thermal connections to achieve a 48-hour hold time and integrated multiplexed TES detector readout. I also validated superconducting readout flex cables for the TIME experiment to inform manufacturing recipes for on-site deployment.

Caltech SURF : Exoplanet Technology Lab

Institution: Caltech  •  Dates: June 2024 – Nov 2025  •  Advisors: Dr. Yinzi Xin, Dr. Nemnja Jovanovic, Prof. Dimitri Mawet

In the Exoplanet Technology Lab at Caltech, I developed post-processing algorithms for the direct imaging of exoplanets. I numerically characterized the PLN's signal limitations and implemented cost-function optimization to successfully localize planets in rotationally non-invariant data. This research culminated in a co-authored paper in JATIS and the submission of a first-author manuscript (currently under review).
